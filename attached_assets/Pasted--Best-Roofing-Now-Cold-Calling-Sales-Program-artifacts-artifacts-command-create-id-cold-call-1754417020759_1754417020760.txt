## Introduction to Cold Calling

### What is Cold Calling in the Roofing Industry?

Cold calling is the practice of contacting potential customers who haven't previously expressed interest in our roofing services. In the roofing industry, cold calling is a powerful tool to:

- Generate new leads
- Identify homeowners with aging or damaged roofs
- Respond quickly to storm-affected areas
- Build awareness of our company in target neighborhoods
- Establish relationships with property owners before they need roofing services

### Why Cold Calling Works for Roofing

The roofing industry is unique because:

1. Homeowners often don't know they need a roof inspection until damage has occurred
2. Many homeowners are unaware they may qualify for insurance coverage for roof damage
3. Property owners frequently don't know how to identify quality roofing contractors
4. Roofing is a high-ticket, infrequent purchase that benefits from education and relationship building

### Cold Calling Ethics at Best Roofing Now

At Best Roofing Now, we approach cold calling with the same integrity and transparency that define all our operations:

- We **never** use high-pressure tactics
- We focus on education and providing value to homeowners
- We honestly assess roof conditions and only recommend services that are truly needed
- We respect homeowners' time and privacy
- We follow all telemarketing regulations and best practices

## Target Audience Identification

### Primary Targets for Cold Calling

1. **Recent Storm Areas**
   - Neighborhoods affected by hail, wind, or severe weather
   - Properties showing visible signs of storm damage

2. **Aging Roof Neighborhoods**
   - Homes built 15-25+ years ago that may need roof replacement
   - Neighborhoods with visible roof deterioration

3. **New Homeowners**
   - Recent home buyers who may need roof assessment
   - Properties that changed hands without roof inspection

4. **Commercial Property Owners**
   - Office buildings, retail spaces, warehouses, etc.
   - Properties with flat or low-slope roofs that require specialized maintenance

### Qualifying Prospects

When calling, quickly assess:

- Property ownership (only speak with property owners or decision-makers)
- Approximate roof age
- Any noticeable roof issues or recent storms
- Previous roofing work history
- Insurance coverage status

## Pre-Call Preparation

### Research and Planning

Before making calls:

1. **Area Research**
   - Identify neighborhoods with specific roofing needs
   - Note recent storm activity in target areas
   - Research average home age and typical roofing materials used

2. **Contact List Preparation**
   - Organize contact information logically by neighborhood
   - Prioritize leads based on likelihood of roofing needs
   - Flag any previous customers or referrals in the area

3. **Timing Considerations**
   - Best calling hours: 9-11 AM and 4-7 PM on weekdays
   - Avoid dinner hours and Sundays
   - Consider calling soon after storms or severe weather

### Call Goals and Metrics

## Cold Calling Scripts

### General Introduction Script

```
Hello, may I speak with [Homeowner Name]?

Hi [Homeowner Name], this is [Your Name] with Best Roofing Now. How are you today?

[Pause for response]

Great! The reason I'm calling is that our inspection team has been in your neighborhood recently, and we've noticed several homes that may need roofing attention due to [recent storm/age/seasonal wear].

We're offering complimentary roof inspections to homeowners in your area this week. Our inspections use advanced drone technology to provide a detailed assessment of your roof's condition without any obligation.

Would you be interested in having one of our certified inspectors take a look at your roof? It takes about 30-45 minutes, and you'll receive a comprehensive report about your roof's condition.

[If yes] Excellent! I have availability on [provide 2-3 options]. Which would work better for your schedule?

[If no] I understand. May I ask if you've had your roof inspected recently? [Engage in conversation to understand their situation]
```

### Post-Storm Script

```
Hello, may I speak with [Homeowner Name]?

Hi [Homeowner Name], this is [Your Name] with Best Roofing Now. I hope you and your family are doing well after the recent [hailstorm/windstorm/severe weather] we had in Charlotte.

The reason I'm calling is that these types of storms can cause roof damage that isn't always visible from the ground but can lead to serious problems down the road. We're currently helping many homeowners in your neighborhood document storm damage for insurance claims.

We're offering free storm damage assessments this week. Our certified inspectors use drone technology to thoroughly document any damage, and if we find issues, we can help you navigate the insurance claims process at no cost to you.

Would you be interested in having us check your roof for storm damage? Most homeowners are surprised to learn their insurance will often cover the cost of storm-related repairs or replacement.

[If yes] Great! I have openings on [provide 2-3 options]. Which would work best for you?

[If no] I understand. Just so you know, storm damage can sometimes take months to show visible signs like leaks. Would it be alright if I check back with you in a few weeks to make sure everything is still looking good?
```

## Handling Common Objections

### "I'm Not Interested"

```
I completely understand. Many of our current customers initially felt the same way. May I ask if you've had your roof inspected recently? The reason I ask is that most roof damage isn't visible from the ground, and catching issues early can save thousands in repairs later.

[Alternative response]

I respect that. Before I let you go, would it be helpful if I sent you some information about how to spot potential roof issues yourself? That way, you'll know when it might be time to call a professional.
```

### "I Already Have a Roofer"

```
That's great! It's important to have a roofer you trust. May I ask when you last had your roof inspected? [If it's been a while] Even with a trusted contractor, it can be valuable to get a second opinion, especially with our free drone inspection technology that provides detailed documentation.

[Alternative response]

I respect loyalty to a good contractor. If you don't mind me asking, have they performed a recent inspection with drone technology? Our advanced inspection process often identifies issues that traditional methods miss, and there's no obligation for the inspection.
```

### "I Can't Afford a New Roof Right Now"

```
I completely understand budget concerns. That's actually why many homeowners appreciate our free inspection service. We can identify if there are any issues that might be covered by your insurance, especially if you've had storm damage. Many of our customers end up only paying their deductible for a complete roof replacement.

[Alternative response]

We're not calling to sell you a new roof today. Our inspection is free and helps you understand the current condition of your roof. If repairs are needed, we offer financing options with payments as low as [current financing offer]. And if the inspection shows your roof is in good shape, you'll have peace of mind at no cost.
```

### "Send Me Some Information"

```
I'd be happy to send you information. Could I ask what specific aspects of roofing you're most interested in learning about? This way, I can make sure to send you the most relevant materials.

[After they respond]

Great, I'll email that to you today. While I have you on the phone, would it be helpful to schedule a quick inspection while our team is in your area this week? It's completely free, and you'll get much more detailed information about your specific roof than any brochure can provide.
```

### "How Much Does a New Roof Cost?"

```
That's a great question. Roof costs vary widely depending on the size of your home, the materials used, and the specific condition of your current roof. Without seeing your roof, I couldn't give you an accurate estimate.

That's why we offer the free inspection - it allows us to provide you with an exact quote based on your specific situation. Many homeowners are also surprised to learn that if there's storm damage, insurance may cover most or all of the cost. Would you like to schedule an inspection so we can give you an accurate quote?
```

## Appointment Setting Techniques

### Creating Urgency (Ethically)

Use these approaches to encourage prompt action:

- "We're in your neighborhood this week only" (when true)
- "We have just a few inspection slots remaining"
- "Storm damage can worsen over time if not addressed"
- "Insurance claims have time limitations after storm events"

### Offering Options Instead of Yes/No Questions

Instead of: "Would you like to schedule an inspection?"

Try: "Would morning or afternoon work better for your inspection?"

### Confirming Appointments

Once scheduled:

1. Repeat the date and time clearly
2. Confirm the property address
3. Ask if both homeowners/decision-makers will be present
4. Explain what to expect during the inspection
5. Provide your contact information
6. Mention you'll send a confirmation email/text
7. Note that the inspector will call 30 minutes before arrival

### Setting Next Steps When Not Ready

If they're not ready to schedule:

1. Establish a specific follow-up date: "Would it be alright if I called you next Thursday to check in?"
2. Offer to send educational information in the meantime
3. Ask if there's a better time to reconnect
4. Provide your direct contact information

## Role-Playing Exercises

### Exercise 1: Basic Introduction

**Scenario**: Cold calling a homeowner in a neighborhood where you've recently completed several projects.

**Goal**: Practice your introduction and value proposition.

**Partners**: Work in pairs, taking turns as caller and homeowner.

**Feedback**: After each role-play, discuss what worked well and what could be improved.

### Exercise 2: Handling Objections

**Scenario**: Homeowner raises specific objections to scheduling an inspection.

**Objections to Practice**:
- "I'm not interested"
- "We just had our roof replaced"
- "How did you get my number?"
- "I can't afford this right now"

**Goal**: Practice responding to objections naturally while moving the conversation forward.

### Exercise 3: Appointment Setting

**Scenario**: Homeowner is interested but hesitant to commit to an appointment.

**Goal**: Practice techniques for securing a firm appointment time.

**Focus Areas**:
- Creating appropriate urgency
- Offering specific time slots
- Confirming all appointment details
- Setting expectations for the inspection

### Exercise 4: Full Call Simulation

**Scenario**: Complete cold call from greeting to close.

**Goal**: Integrate all elements of an effective call into a natural conversation.

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Confidence and tone
- Response to homeowner cues
- Effective questioning
- Objection handling
- Close/next steps
